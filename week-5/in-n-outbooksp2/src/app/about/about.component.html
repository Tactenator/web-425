Who was Kurt Vonnegut?

Kurt Vonnegut was an American writer known for his satirical and darkly humorous novels. He is a well published author with over fourteen novels to his name as well as other various forms of literature. At first a student, he would later enlist in the army where he would be deployed to Europe to fight in WWII. He was captured and placed in an internment camp in Dresden. He would be rescued and returned home where he began his journey as an author. His first book, Player Piano, was met with little commercial success. It wasn't until his future novels that he would become critically acclaimed and would be nominated for multiple Hugo Awards. Kurt Vonnegut passed away in 2007 at the age of 84.
